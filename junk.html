Paul Valincort

  • Play paranoid
  • Say something about what your partner just said
    1. what did they literally say
    2. what do I think that means
    3. how do i think or feel about what they said
    4. what am i going to do about it
  • Press your partner's buttons. "Don't tell me I'm tall" TELL THEM
  • You should be in your head some. We are not just actors. The improviser steers a character they don't just ride them. make your character do the fun things
  • If a scene seems like it has an obvious conclusion like a seduction scene, remember it is how we get there that is fun. enrich the journey
  • Dramatic characters are complex. Comedic characters are simple. If you have a hammer treat everything like a nail. If you are supportive, support the guy robbing you
  • Say something about your partner's character. Something about what they think or feel
  • If you're stuck start a sentence with "here's the thing". it will force you to address something important
  • confessions work the same way
  • Say yes and to the other improviser, not exactly the character. Say yes to the game move
  • The relationship is the transaction; the activity is the currency. We use what we are doing to advance the relationship
  • No one ever left a show and thought, "man... they really fixed that bike"
  • Know everything, have everything. Not having what they ask for is a dead end. Knowing a wrong answer is fun, not knowing is the end
  • Say yes in spite of your character. If your character is afraid of heights, its your job as the improviser to find a reason for her to say yes to climbing a mountain
  • Don't let your character get in the way of the scene
  • Be vulnerable to change
  • Have, Say, Do
